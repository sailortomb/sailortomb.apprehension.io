STAR TREK ADVENTURES - CAPTAIN'S LOG

U.S.S. APPREHENSION • MISSION 01 • ACT 1 (Scenes 1-5)

Act 1 - First Officer’s Log, Stardate 57103.0.

The Apprehension has been ordered to the Romulan edge of the Neutral Zone in a mission of humanitarian relief for a people known as the Ehtahni. Our contacts in the Romulan government describe a situation of a mysterious and deadly disease that has run rampant, leading to serious social unrest. Starfleet has chosen to respond and render whatever aid we can bring. Intelligence indicates a society on the verge of collapse, something we will do our very best to prevent. 

Captain Muroc tells me that he views our orders as a sign of trust and respect, and that our superiors are acknowledging our inherent value by putting us to task with important work right from the get-go. I am inclined to agree, though I wonder if that sentiment is shared throughout the crew. Many of the Apprehension’s compliment are recent graduates of the Starfleet Science Academy and know a hell of a lot more about anomalies and intermix chambers than they do about border politics and the Neutral Zone. I am confident that the senior staff will tune them to a nicety: we’ve got a very skilled team assembled here.

Chief Science Officer S’trov S’kir and Captain Muroc served together on the USS Diligent, a ship that saw a great deal of action during the war, and the easy way our Caitian scientist smirks around the Captain’s logic and rhetoric bodes well. If I didn’t know better I’d say the Captain actually enjoys Mr. S’kir’s banter.

I haven’t spent much time with the rest of  our science crew yet, but I look forward to seeing what they can do with our upgraded systems. They’re a smart department, and this is one hell of a smart ship.

Chief Engineer Manfred Devi has been very helpful in the last few days getting myself  and the rest of the bridge crew get as familiar as possible with the Apprehension’s systems. Anyone who spent time in a medicine ball during the war knows just how valuable the Olympics were, but this version is something unique. To be honest it’s been a challenge bringing my expectations up to the level we’ll be flying. I mentioned this to Chief Devi and he told me that 'Looks can be deceiving'. He’s been onboard since the refit began two years ago and knows the ship like the back of his hand. Good guy. A bit snobby about the engine but what Chief isn’t? Keeping two reaction chambers running smoothly requires a gordian knot of a duty roster and I think we’ll have our hands full. Good thing we get along. 

We were designed and shipped to plumb the mysteries of the galaxy but I firmly believe one of the deepest ones is onboard and running our Medical division. Dr. Daval Radzik is as captivating as a super nova, and as unfathomable as a quantum singularity. I’m not unfamiliar with the personalities of Joined Trill by any means - they tend to traffic in popular circles on my homeworld where a certain volume of experience is particularly valued - but I have yet to catch her in an unguarded moment of conversation. Almost everyone on board had a chance to make her acquaintance during the standard profile building bass-line exams after we set out and there have been no complaints. Call it a Risian stereotype but I like to be on close terms with the commanding staff. I think Dr. Radzik will be a challenge, but one that I’m pleased to take on. 

I am pleased to note that the Captain took my recommendation for the Chief of Security posting and Lt. CMDR B’Lesh will be serving with us on this voyage. I first met the young Klingon xB three years ago while volunteering at the Van'Lavan Cybernetics Rehab Institute on Risa and was struck by her strength and resilience. She had a potential I knew Starfleet would hone into a valuable officer and it looks like I was right. Her service record shows that she’s kept up with the Vulcan tennis so I figure I should practice a little before the inevitable callout.

We’re currently on target to reach the Ehtahn system in twelve hours time, which I plan to use brushing up on local custom and tradition. The diplomatic package we received paints the Ehtahni as a people who are loyal to their government and history. They have warp technology and aren’t afraid to use it but have only explored a few lights around their home star, so not particularly prone to adventure. If it weren’t for their current disaster it is entirely likely they would have remained unacquainted with Starfleet for a very long time. I’m going to do everything in my power as Commander to ensure this is a good first impression.

End Log.

-Scene 01-

The Apprehension enters the Ehtahni system at the edge of its heliosphere, advancing towards Ehtahni Prime on impulse while exchanging subspace communication with their envoy in the local government, a man named Li’am Tulloko. He confirms that the situation on Ehtahn is dire: multiple regional governments have fallen and the planetary body is struggling to slow the spread and maintain quarantine. Captain Muroc tries to placate the envoy by reassuring them that the Apprehension and its crew are ready to help in whatever way they can.

@ Does the Captain manage to placate the Ehtahni Envoy?
d: Yes/No ’Somewhat Probable’ 1d20=7 (SUCCESS)
=> Envoy Tulloko breathes out a sigh of relief at the Captain’s words and voices a heartfelt thanks.

The desperation of the situation is clearly taking its toll. I inform Tulloko that the Apprehension is on route and can be expected in an hour or so. He thanks us and closes comms.

Captain Muroc instructs me that the Science and Medical departments are to expect visitors from the surface shortly after we arrive and to prepare accordingly. Taking my place at my personal Command Conn I contact the chiefs and pass the orders to Dr. S’Kir and Dr. Radzik. They mostly know what to expect but they’ll have their hands full from here on out, and would likely appreciate the heads up. I know I would. 

@ Do the other department heads appreciate Cmdr. Karmak's heads up?
d: Yes/No ’Probable’ 2d20=7 (YES - S'Kir) 20 (NO - Radzik)
=> Note: Since this is a 2d20 Matrix roll the 20 does NOT generate threat.
=> Dr. S’kir returns my comm and tells me they’re all bright-eyed and bushy-tailed down there. The slightly higher pitch hum of the ancillary reactor pulsing faintly in the background. Dr. Radzik merely returns with a brief “Acknowledged,” before the comm chirps closed.

Having extended an olive branch of sorts to the two doctors, I return my attention to the cultural brief displayed on my personal conn. The Ehtahni are said to value respect and honesty and strive to maintain a good quality of life for all of their people. With this medical disaster threatening the fabric of their society I can only imagine how the nerves of the government are frayed, to say nothing of the every-day people who depend on them.

-Scene 02-

As the Apprehension approaches the planet and begins to descend into a geosynchronous orbit around the capital city of Key’Haht, I instruct the science team to begin a detailed biological scan of the population of the city, putting to use the enhanced sensors our ship’s saucer-sphere touts on its underside. 

@ Are the enhanced instruments able to tell us anything more about the state of health on the surface?
d: Sensors + Science 11+5=16 2d20=12,17 (SUCCESS)
=> The sensors on the ship are able to take high resolution bio scans of the Ehtahni on the surface across the most populated continent and the science team generates a detailed precis. The facts are indeed made clearer, if not less troubling.

The sensitive instruments of the vessel extend from their pits and direct their gaze at the planet bellow. Once they have made several detailed sweeps of the capital, the command crew convenes in the observation lounge behind the bridge.

The room and primary seating are shaped in a familiar delta formation. At the widest part of the table, a holo grid faintly flickers. Dr. S’kir taps a short sequence on his PADD and displays the results on a rotating triangular column of screens. Even without a serious background in medical I can tell from the readouts that the situation is dire. After a moment Dr. Radzik confirms our fears, the rate of infection is spreading, and the measures the Ehtahni have taken are about to be overwhelmed.

A map replaces the readouts. It shows us that the regions where infected people concentrate is a writhing mass of red with only a few blue 'cold spots' of healthy beings remaining. According to the sensor analysis these boroughs are being fiercely partitioned into to small sterile zones under a judicious guard, while the other areas have been left to fend for themselves as best they can with dwindling resources. From the diplomatic brief we know that the people here are loyal to their government, but my education in sociology tells me that pressure like this can cause even the calmest civilizations to ignite in sudden, shocking violence.

I concentrate on the information think about the situation down there.

@ Knowing what I do about populations like this, does any strategy come mind?
d: Control + Science=15, 2d20=6/4 (SUCCESS - Momentum gained)
=> The pockets of healthy Ehtahni and the walls keeping them from the infected populis will create targets of opportunity for the desperate. Some spots will be worse than others, and with complex data provided we might be able to quickly model problem locations before violence errupts.

Pointing out the cool areas on the readout, I suggest that we may be able to initiate more successful quarantine techniques by predicting where major outbreaks are most likely to occur in the short term after cross-referencing data from the surface. Feeling as though I understand the situation more now than I did before, I note a three specific healthy zones ringing the capital that - in my experience - would be most likely to see violence based on the factors we observed.

Captain Muroc nods and leaves the communication of this element of the plan to Doctor Radzik and myself once the delegates have arrived. I glance to meet her eye and receive the barest of calm nods. It's a start.

The conn from the bridge interjects and informs the captain that the Ehtahni delegation is ready to beam over. Captain Muroc acknowledges the message and stands from his seat, raising an eyebrow to Dr. Radzik and myself, he takes us up in his wake and we make our way to the nearest turbo lift to meet Envoy Tulloko and the retinue from the surface.
